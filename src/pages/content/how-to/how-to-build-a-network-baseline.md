Most outages aren’t caused by failures — they’re caused by unknowns.

A network baseline gives you a clear picture of what “normal” looks like so you can quickly identify what’s wrong when things break.

## Why This Matters

Clients don’t care about packet captures or routing tables — they care about uptime, performance, and predictability.

A baseline gives them all three.

## Step-by-Step Guide

1. Inventory your devices  
2. Capture interface statistics  
3. Document routing tables  
4. Record CPU/memory usage  
5. Establish thresholds  
6. Store everything centrally  

## Common Mistakes

- Not updating the baseline regularly  
- Only capturing partial data  
- Not documenting changes  

## Summary

A baseline is the foundation of every stable network. Build it once, maintain it regularly, and your troubleshooting time drops dramatically.
